Southwest Coder Dojo

Welcome!

Southwest Coder Dojo is a coding club for teens who want to learn computer programming. We are part of an international organization of coding clubs.

At Dojo meetings mentors work with small groups on an online tutorial in a language like HTML, CSS or Python.

Register to attend--seats are limited.

No experience in programming required!

Why learn to code?

It's fun, and creative! Make websites, apps, games and more...

It's an essential skill, like reading or writing.

Learning to code can lead to a great job!

Events

Dojo meetings are intended for beginners. At the Dojo we'll work through an online tutorial in a programming language such as HTLML, CSS or Python. If you have questions about a personal coding project, mentors will be available to help towards the end of the meeting.

Register to reserve a ticket and bring your laptop, charger and earbuds.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Dojo?

It's a club where kids learn to code.

How old do you have to be to participate?

Our Dojo is for teens ages 13-18.

What if my parents want to come along?

Parents can find a comfy place to wait for you at the library. Since parents are not participants, they do not need tickets.

Who are the volunteers?

Our mentors are members of the community with some coding experience. Many are parents of Southwest High School students. All adult volunteers have background checks on file.

What should I bring to a Dojo meeting?

Bring your laptop, power charger and earbuds.

About Southwest Coder Dojo

Southwest Coder Dojo is a member of Coder Dojo International. It was founded in partnership with Southwest High School to provide introductory coding experiences for teens in the Southwest Minneapolis area.

Many of the volunteers are Southwest High School parents and include professional software developers and software industry professionals from local companies.
