Welcome!

Southwest Coder Dojo is a coding club for teens who want to learn computer programming in Southwest Minneapolis. We are members of Coder Dojo International, an organization of free, volunteer-led coding clubs.

At Dojo meetings mentors work with small groups on an online lesson in a language like HTML, CSS, JavaScript or Python.

Register to attend! Tickets are free, but seats are limited.

No experience in programming required!

Why learn to code?

It's fun and creative! Make websites, apps, games and more...

It's an essential skill, like reading or writing.

Learning to code can lead to a great job!

Register for Dojo Events

Dojo meetings are intended for beginners and those without prior coding experience. At the Dojo we'll separate into small groups to work through online tutorials in a programming languages such as HTLML, CSS, JavaScript or Python based on your interests.

If you don't know what language you'd like to learn, we'll help you decide. For those that need help with a personal coding project, mentors will be available to help towards the end of the meeting.

Register to reserve a ticket and bring your laptop, charger and earbuds.

Frequently Asked Questions


What is a Dojo?

It's a club where kids learn to code.

How old do you have to be to participate?

Our Dojo is for teens ages 13-18.

What if my parents want to come along?

Parents can find a comfy place to wait for you at the library. Since parents are not participants, they do not need tickets.

Who are the volunteers?

Our mentors are members of the community with some coding experience. Many are parents of Southwest High School students. All adult volunteers have background checks on file.

What should I bring to a Dojo meeting?

Bring your laptop, power charger and earbuds.

About Southwest Coder Dojo

Southwest Coder Dojo is a member of Coder Dojo International. It was founded with the idea of providing introductory coding experiences for teens in the Southwest Minneapolis area.

Many of the volunteers are parents of current and former Southwest High School students and include professional software developers and software industry professionals from the Twin Cities.
